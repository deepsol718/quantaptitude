Time and Work Logic

Unitary method/ Chocolate Method

We have learned chocolate method to solve problems of time and work. Let understand the logic behind this method.

Lets take an example of two persons, A takes 15 days and B takes 10 days to complete a work.
now, we have to calculate the days required to do the same amount of work if they start working together?
solution = first take the number of days that that can be easily divisible by each workers no of days i.e.
find LCM of the two.
for our case it is 30.
now, if A start working for 30 days. he will complete that work 2 times. similarly B will complete 3 times of that work.
if both started together they would have completed the work 5 times in 30 days. so, to check the number of days they will
take to complete the work once would be simply divide 30 by 5 i.e. 6 days.

Lets take a look at what we have done.
steps to calculate no of days worker take if they start together:
1. calculate the LCM.
2. calculate the number of times individual worker can complete that work in LCM days.
3. add the number of times of work of each worker which will give total numbers of times a worker will do work if they
started together and work of LCM days.
4. divide the LCM by total no of times.
5. the result of step 4 will give u the numbers of days total worker will take to work to complete the work once.

Note: We calculated the LCM to make our calculation easy.
we can take any number of days but taking LCM is the simplest way to do it.
